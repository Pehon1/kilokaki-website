How to Read Your Weekly Summary

KiloKaki sends a weekly summary every Sunday. Here's what the numbers actually tell you.

Your weekly summary is your macro report card. It shows what you ate over the past 7 days compared to your daily targets — so you can see patterns, not just single meals.

How to Get It

KiloKaki sends the weekly summary automatically on Sunday. You can also request it anytime:

Text message "weekly summary" or "show me the report"

What the Summary Shows

1

Daily averages

Your average calories and macros per day over the week. Not a sum — a per-day number, which is what matters when comparing to your daily targets.

2

Days on target vs. off target

How many days you hit your calorie target (within a reasonable range, e.g. ±10%) and how many you overshot or undershot.

3

Macro split

Your average protein, carbs, and fat as a percentage of total calories. Useful for checking whether your macro split aligns with your goal — maintenance, cut, or recomposition.

4

Logging streak

How many consecutive days you logged. Consistency is the strongest predictor of progress — not perfection, just showing up.

💡 Don't read too much into any single day. The weekly view smooths out the noise. One bad day in a 6-day good week is noise. Six bad days is a pattern — time to investigate.

Using the Summary

  • Compare this week to last week — are you trending toward your goal?
  • Spot macro imbalances — if carbs are consistently high but protein is low, adjust your food choices
  • Identify trigger days — Wednesday and Friday spikes often point to social eating
