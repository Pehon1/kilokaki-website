How to Log from a Nutrition Label

Take a photo of the nutrition label. KiloKaki reads the per-serving values so you don't have to.

Packaged food makes tracking easier — the nutrition label tells you exactly what's in one serving. Instead of typing all those numbers, just photograph the label and let KiloKaki do the work.

Step by Step

1

Photograph the nutrition label

Use the paperclip icon in Telegram and select a photo of the nutrition label. Flat labels (on the back of packages) photograph best. Make sure the text is legible in the frame.

2

Send the photo

Tap send. KiloKaki reads the per-serving values automatically — calories, protein, carbs, fat, and sodium from the label.

3

Add serving context (optional)

If you ate more or less than one serving, add a caption to adjust:

Caption "ate 2 servings"
Caption "half packet only"
💡 This works great for supplements, protein bars, cereal, yogurt, and any packaged food you track regularly. One photo, no manual entry.

Works Best With

  • Nutrition facts panels on packaged food
  • Supplement labels (protein powders, vitamin drinks)
  • Ready-to-drink beverages with calorie/macro info

KiloKaki reads the "per serving" values from the label. If you ate multiple servings, tell it in the caption and it will multiply accordingly.
