How to Adjust Portions

Tell KiloKaki how many servings you ate. It does the math.

Nutrition databases give you macro values per 100g or per serving. KiloKaki lets you scale those values to match exactly how much you ate — no spreadsheet required.

How It Works

After you log a food item, reply to that entry with a portion multiplier or a specific amount. KiloKaki recalculates the macros from the base values.

Step by Step

1

Log the food as usual

Send a voice note, photo, or text — whatever method you prefer. KiloKaki returns a macro estimate based on a standard portion.

2

Reply with your portion adjustment

Reply to a log entry "2.5 servings"
Reply to a log entry "ate 1 and a half"
Reply to a log entry "200g only"
Reply to a log entry "half portion"
3

KiloKaki updates the macros

The macro values are recalculated and your daily total adjusts accordingly.

💡 The adjustment works even on photo logs. If KiloKaki identified "grilled chicken rice" and you ate 2 portions, reply "2 bowls" and it'll scale all values up.

What You Can Say

  • "2.5 servings", "3 servings", "1.5" — any fraction or whole number
  • "double" or "twice" — scales to 2x
  • "half portion" or "only half"
  • "200g" or "300g" — specify exact weight if you know it
  • "skipped the rice" or "no sides" — removes an item from a composite dish
