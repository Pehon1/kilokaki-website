How to Log Food by Voice

Speak your meal. KiloKaki calculates the macros. No typing, no scrolling, no friction.

What You Need

The KiloKaki Telegram bot. That's it. If you haven't started, tap Open Bot → and send your first voice message.

Step by Step

1

Hold the microphone button

In Telegram, hold the mic button next to the text field. The bot is listening for a voice message — not a text command.

2

Say what you ate

Speak naturally. Include quantity and food description. Be as specific as you can — the more detail, the more accurate the macro estimate.

Voice message (transcribed) "1 bowl of char kway teow with bean sprouts"
3

Wait 2–3 seconds

KiloKaki processes the audio, maps it to macro data, and replies with a nutrition breakdown. You'll see calories, protein, carbs, fat, and sodium — auto-calculated from your daily target.

💡 KiloKaki uses USDA food database references to estimate macros. Speak the specific dish name if you know it — "char kway teow" gets a better estimate than "fried noodles."

What Good Voice Logs Sound Like

Voice message (transcribed) "2 eggs over easy 1 slice whole wheat toast with butter"
Voice message (transcribed) "300 grams of grilled salmon 1 cup brown rice half a cucumber"

When Voice Doesn't Work

If KiloKaki misidentifies a dish, you can edit the log afterward. Voice gets you close fast — precision comes from correction.

That's the whole interaction. One voice note → instant macro data. No app switching, no manual search, no typing mistakes.
