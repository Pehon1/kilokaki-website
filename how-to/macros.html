How to Track Your Macros

Beyond calories — protein, carbs, fat, and sodium. What each one means and why each matters.

Macros — short for macronutrients — are the three nutrients your body needs in large amounts: protein, carbohydrates, and fat. Sodium is tracked separately because excess sodium matters for health outcomes even if it doesn't have calories.

The Four Numbers KiloKaki Tracks

P

Protein (g)

The building block of muscle. Most people undereat protein relative to their activity level. If you're training, cutting, or over 40, prioritise it. Target: 1.6–2.2g per kg of bodyweight for active people.

C

Carbohydrates (g)

Your body's preferred fuel for high-intensity activity. Rice, bread, fruit, sugar — all carbs. Not inherently bad. What matters is how much you eat relative to your activity level.

F

Fat (g)

Essential for hormone production and nutrient absorption. Don't fear it. Around 0.7–1g per kg of bodyweight is a common range. Focus on getting fat from whole sources rather than processed oils.

Na

Sodium (mg)

Not a macro, but tracked separately because most people eat well over the recommended 2,300mg/day. If you're eating processed or restaurant food regularly, sodium adds up. Watch it if blood pressure is a concern.

Checking Your Macros

Type /today to see your current daily totals and how much you have left for each macro.

You can also see your running totals after every log entry — KiloKaki shows you calories and macros consumed vs your targets each time.

💡 Calorie-only tracking misses the point. Two people can eat the same calories but very different macro splits — and get very different body composition results. Log the macros, not just the calories.
