How to Set Your Daily Targets

Tell KiloKaki your goal. It calculates your calorie target and gives you macro targets to match.

KiloKaki needs to know where you're going so it can tell you how close you are. Your daily targets are the finish line every log entry gets measured against.

Step by Step

1

Tell KiloKaki your goal

Start with the basic: your target calories and what you're trying to achieve.

Text message "set calories to 1800" or "target 2000 calories maintenance"
Text message "I want to lose weight, set my target"
2

Set your macro targets (optional)

You can set each macro individually or let KiloKaki suggest a split based on your calorie target and goal.

Text message "set protein to 150g, carbs to 200g, fat to 60g"
Text message "suggest a macro split for me"
3

KiloKaki confirms

Your targets are saved. Every time you log, KiloKaki shows you remaining macros for the day — a live progress bar against your goal.

What to Set

  • Calories: Start here. Use a TDEE calculator online to estimate your maintenance, then subtract 300–500kcal for a cut or add for a bulk.
  • Protein: Most important macro. 1.6–2.2g per kg bodyweight if active; 1.2–1.6g if sedentary.
  • Carbs: Flexible. Higher carb for active days, lower for rest days.
  • Fat: Don't drop below 0.5g per kg bodyweight — essential for hormone health.
  • Sodium: Most people aim for under 2,300mg. If you eat out often, check it.
💡 You can update your targets anytime. As you lose weight, your calorie needs change — recalculate every 4–6 weeks or after any significant bodyweight change.
