How to Add Text Context to Photos

Photo + caption = accurate macros. Here's how to use both together.

A photo alone tells KiloKaki what you're eating. Adding a caption tells it how much. Together, you get an accurate macro estimate without manual lookup.

Why a Caption Helps

KiloKaki identifies the dish from the photo using food recognition. But portion size is hard to judge from an image — is that a small bowl or a large? Half a plate or a full one?

A caption like "1.5 servings" or "half portion" tells KiloKaki exactly how to scale the macro data.

Step by Step

1

Select your photo

Tap the attachment icon in Telegram. Choose a photo of your meal. Don't send it yet.

2

Add your caption

Telegram lets you add a caption below the photo before sending. Type your context here — quantity and any modifiers.

Caption on photo "1.5 servings chicken rice"
Caption on photo "half portion noodles no bone"
Caption on photo "2.5 slices pizza"
3

Send

Tap send. KiloKaki reads both the photo and the caption together, giving you a portion-adjusted macro estimate.

💡 Even vague context helps: "bigger portion", "smaller than usual", "skipped the rice" — KiloKaki uses whatever you give it to improve the estimate.

What to Write in the Caption

  • Quantity: "2 bowls", "1.5 servings", "3 slices"
  • Modifications: "no skin", "no rice", "extra sauce"
  • Preparation: "grilled", "deep fried", "steamed"
  • Brand or specific dish name: "McSpicy single no bun" or "Zhong Guo Mahua chicken rice"
