# 1. FEATURES SECTION

## Section header

## Hero feature: Photo Logging (lead)

**Headline:** Your plate is the input. That's it.

**Body:**
Send a photo of anything on your plate — chicken rice, cai fan, nasi lemak, whatever you actually eat. KiloKaki identifies the food, estimates portions, and logs your macros. No searching databases. No weighing. No app to download.

Accuracy sits at 85–90% on common Singapore meals. When it's wrong, edit it in one tap.

**Mini proof:** 22.5% of photo logs get a portion adjustment in one tap — not a rewrite, just a nudge.

**Works especially well for:**
- Hawker centre plates
- Restaurant meals
- Meal prep containers
- Anything you're eating right now

## Feature 2: Voice Logging

**Headline:** Speak it. Log it. Move on.

**Body:**
Hold the mic. Say what you ate — "grilled chicken breast with brown rice and some greens" or "economy rice with two dishes." KiloKaki transcribes it, detects the food, and logs your macros. No typing. No searching. No app switching.

You speak 3x faster than you type. Voice logging exists for the moments when your hands are full, you're in a rush, or you're walking out the door.

**Works especially well for:**
- Hawker queues
- Lunch at your desk
- Meals eaten with others
- Anything you can't photograph cleanly

**Note:** Voice is a genuine differentiator. No other food tracker in Telegram does this.

---

## Feature 3: Text Logging

**Headline:** Type it. We'll figure it out.

**Body:**
Describe your meal in plain English. "Chicken rice lunch" or "protein shake with banana" or "2 slices pepperoni pizza, large." The parser handles it.

Text logging covers the meals that don't get photographed — leftovers, desk snacks, late-night eats. It also works when you need to be quiet or when voice recognition struggles with background noise.

**Works especially well for:**
- Simple, single-dish entries
- Specific ingredient tracking
- Quiet environments
- Quick corrections after photo/voice

---

## Feature 4: Daily Macro Tracking

**Headline:** See the whole picture, every day.

**Body:**
Every log updates your daily totals. Calories, protein, carbs, fat, sodium — against whatever targets you've set. No manual calculation. No spreadsheet.

When you're close to your goals, you'll get a nudge. When you're way off, you'll see that too. Trends build over time, not just in the moment.

---

## Feature 5: Edit & Delete

**Headline:** Your log, your rules.

**Body:**
Logged the wrong thing? Forgot something? Changed your mind about portion size? Edit anything, any time. Send `/edit` or tap the edit button — most adjustments take under 10 seconds.

If you logged something by mistake, delete it. Full stop. No guilt, no friction. Your log is yours.

---

## Feature 6: History & Analytics

**Headline:** Know what's actually working.

**Body:**
Look back at any day. See what you logged, what your totals were, and how you tracked against your goals. Spot patterns — which meals push you over, which days you consistently under-eat protein, when your sodium spikes.

Data that's invisible can't help you. History makes it visible.

---

## Feature 7: Habit Reminders

**Headline:** Logging without being annoying about it.

**Body:**
Set a daily reminder — whatever time works for you. KiloKaki sends a nudge, not a lecture. If you're already done for the day, ignore it. If you haven't logged yet, one tap opens the bot.

Users who set reminders log more consistently. Users who log consistently hit their goals more often. The connection is direct.

# 2. FAQ SECTION

## Header: Questions, answered.

---

**Q: Is KiloKaki really free?**
Yes. No subscription. No ads. No "pro" tier hiding important features. It's in beta, which means it's functional and we're still improving it — but you don't pay for access.

---

**Q: Do I need to download an app?**
No. KiloKaki runs inside Telegram. If you have Telegram, you have KiloKaki. Open the bot, start logging. That's the whole onboarding.

---

**Q: Does it work for hawker food?**
Yes — and that's the whole point. Chicken rice, cai fan, nasi lemak, bak chor mee, mee siam, Yong Tau Foo, economy rice — KiloKaki is built for the meals people in Singapore actually eat, not for salads and smoothie bowls.

---

**Q: How accurate is it?**
85–90% on common Singapore meals. Accuracy is highest for recognizable dishes with standard preparations — hawker plates, restaurant mains, common packaged foods. The more unusual the dish, the more the estimate may vary.

When it's off, a one-tap edit sets it right. No re-logging from scratch.

---

**Q: Can I edit a log after I've saved it?**
Yes. Send `/edit` or tap the edit button. Most edits are portion adjustments — done in seconds, no friction.

---

**Q: What if I logged the wrong thing?**
Delete it. Full stop. No judgment, no confirmation loops. Your log is yours to manage.

---

**Q: Does it track sodium?**
Yes. Calories, protein, carbs, fat, and sodium — all five, every log, every day.

---

**Q: Can I use nutrition labels from packaged food?**
Yes. For packaged foods with readable nutrition labels, KiloKaki can read and use the facts directly.

---

**Q: Is my data private?**
Your logs are stored in our system so you can access them across sessions and devices. We don't sell them, share them with third parties, or use them for anything other than running the service.

---

**Q: What's the difference between KiloKaki and MyFitnessPal?**
MyFitnessPal is a database search app — you find the entry, you log it, you move on. KiloKaki removes the search step entirely. Photo a plate, speak a meal, or type a description. The AI handles identification and portion estimation. No searching. No guessing which entry matches.

It's also built for Singapore food first, not as an afterthought.

---

**Q: Can I use KiloKaki outside Singapore?**
Yes. KiloKaki works anywhere. It was built for Singapore and Asian food, but it handles global cuisines too — pasta, burgers, stir-fries, curries. If you can describe it or photograph it, it can log it.

---

**Q: How do I get started?**
Open [t.me/KiloKakiBot](https://t.me/KiloKakiBot) in Telegram. Send a photo, a voice note, or a text description of something you ate. That's the entire onboarding.

# 5. ABOUT PAGE

## Header: What we're building and why.

## Who it's for
KiloKaki is for people who want to track what they eat without the overhead of a traditional food diary. You know you should log your meals. You know it would help. But the friction of searching databases, weighing food, and filling out forms kills the habit before it starts.

We're building the tool you'd actually use.

## The problem we solved for ourselves
Most calorie apps are built for people eating salads, grilled chicken breast, and protein shakes. They assume you're preparing food in a kitchen with a food scale. They break when you eat at a hawker centre, split a dish with someone, or grab something random from a coffee shop.

KiloKaki was built by people who eat chicken rice and cai fan. It understands the food we actually eat. That sounds obvious. It wasn't.

## How it works (the short version)
You send a photo, voice note, or text description of your meal through Telegram. KiloKaki identifies the food, estimates portions, and logs your macros. Your daily totals update automatically. You can edit anything, any time. Your history is stored so you can see your patterns over time.

## What's coming
- Better accuracy on mixed dishes and regional specialties
- Expanded language support for voice logging
- Deeper analytics on trends and patterns
- Meal planning tools that work with your food log

We're building in public. The blog is where we share what we're working on.

## The team
KiloKaki is a small team based in Singapore. We build in Telegram because that's where people already are. We keep it free because tracking food should not require a subscription.

Questions? Reach us through the bot or at [hello@kilokaki.com](mailto:hello@kilokaki.com).
